SEO is not dying.

But if you're still doing SEO the same way you were three years ago, you're optimising for a search engine that is quietly being replaced.

For years, the goal was simple. Rank on page one. Get the click. Drive the traffic.

That game is changing faster than most people in our industry are willing to admit.

Google's AI Overviews are answering questions directly on the results page ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Claude are becoming the first place people go when they want a recommendation or a quick answer. 

And just last week, Google officially documented google-agent, a framework that signals how deeply agentic AI is being woven into how search works going forward.

People are getting answers without ever clicking a link.

Which means ranking number one on Google is no longer the whole story.
The question is no longer just "can Google find my page."

It's "when someone asks an AI for a recommendation in my industry, does my brand show up in the answer?"

That's Generative Engine Optimisation. And it's not a replacement for SEO. It's the next layer on top of it.

The fundamentals still matter. A technically sound site. Strong content. Real authority in your space. These are still the foundation.

But the brands that win in the next three years will be the ones that started thinking beyond the blue links early enough.

I see too many people handing over their strategy keys to AI.

You wake up, open ChatGPT or Claude, and the first prompt looks something like this:

"I would like to promote our new SKU for men's shoes in Nairobi. Help me with a marketing strategy that drives sales and include 10 post ideas."

Oh well... good luck with that.

Sasa here is the hard truth, AI is fast math. 

It is not insight.

9/10 times, when you prompt like this, you get a random blurb of "best practices" regurgitated from 2021. 

And because the LLM was trained by people smarter than us, it sounds convincing. 

It fools you into thinking you have a plan.

But as I’ve learned from digging into AI and having worked in marketing for close to a decade, there is a massive gap between "output" and "outcome."

Here is why prompting to get a marketing strategy fails terribly:

❌ AI lacks Brand Nuance. It doesn’t know your history, your values, or why your last campaign flopped. 
❌ It lacks Customer Intimacy: It doesn’t know that your specific customer in Nairobi buys based on durability, not fashion (or vice versa). 
❌ It creates a "Sea of Sameness": If your competitor types that same prompt, they get the same answer. And it shows with the 1 thousand captions that all look the same on our feeds each day😂

I'm not saying don't use AI. Just note that AI is a powerful tool. It amplifies your work.
